How electricity is made

Batteries are an critical in powering our everyday devices, from mobile phones and laptops, to watches and hearing aids. Batteries use a chemical reaction to convert chemical energy into electrical energy.

However, most of the electricity we use everyday comes from the power company. This electrical energy is readily available through outlets around our homes, and heats our home, runs our computers, cooks our food, lights our rooms, and many more things... Where does the energy come from to do this, and how does it get to our homes?

There are a number of different ways that the power company provides electricity, but the most common way is through an electric generator. A generator is a device that converts mechanical energy (movement) into electrical energy. So, to generate electricity, all you need to do is provide the mechanical energy to turn the generator. For example, if you actuually turn the generator by hand, then you can generate electricty.

Funky magnets. How do they work?

It turns out that magnetism and electricty and are two tightly intertwined related concepts. Magnets can generate electric current, and likewise, electric currents can generate the affects of a magnet. When a magnet moves nears a closed loop of conducting wire, a current is generated in the loop. And the reverse is true as well: an electric current near a magnetc will push the magnet.

Both of these concepts are used in two very similar items:

  • In an generator a loop of wire rotated around a magnet generates a current in that wire.

  • In an electric motor a current in a loop of wire causes a magnet to turn.

Electric motors are used everywhere - they spin our washing machines, they run our air conditioning and refrigerators, pump our water, and even turn the wheels on electric vehicles.

The primary way to mechanically turn a generate is to push steam through a turbine (which is similar to a fan). The steam pushes the blades of the turbine, which turns the generator to generate electricy. So how do you generate steam? You need to boil water, which you can do in many ways:

  • Burn something. This is the primary way generating electrical energy in the U.S. The main fuel we burn is coal, followed by natural gas.

  • Nuclear reactors use a process of "splitting" atoms which releases vasts amount of energy. This energy can be used to boil water.

  • Solar energy: Mirros and lenses can reflect the sun's light to a small spot that geneartes huge amount of heat.

So that is how electricity is generated - but how does it get to the electronics in your home. The power company relies on a vast network of interconnected wires (often called "the grid") that deliver electrical energy to you and your neighbors homes.
