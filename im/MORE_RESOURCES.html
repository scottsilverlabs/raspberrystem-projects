Connecting to a Network

There are likely going to be times when you're going to want to connect your RaspberrySTEM CREATOR Kit to the Internet. For example, you may want to download a new version of the RaspberrySTEM software or you may be interested in getting additional Raspberry Pi projects that you want to implement on the RaspberrySTEM.

There are two ways to connect your RaspberrySTEM CREATOR Kit to the Internet:

Wired Connectivity

Of the two connectivity options, Wired Connectivity is typically going to be the easiest, assuming you have access to a router that is connected to the Internet. Internet routers are standard if you have a home or business Internet connection through your phone company or cable company, and will likely be located at the point where the cable or phone lines come into your house.

Getting Internet connectivity on your RaspberrySTEM CREATOR Kit is typically as simple as plugging one side of an Ethernet cable (not included with the kit) into the Ethernet port on the back of the Raspberry Pi (the connector is located next to the USB connectors where your keyboard and mouse are plugged in) and then plugging the other side of the Ethernet cable into your Internet-connected router.

Pic of Pi connected to router

While this connectivity method should often work without any additional configuration, if you find that you're having issues, you should verify that your router is configured to use DHCP (Dynamic Host Configuration Protocol). This is the way your router identifies and adds new devices to your network, and is a default setting for most routers these days.

Wireless Connectivity

If it's not possible to connect your Raspberry Pi to the Internet using a hard-wired connection to your router (for example, if you don't have access to your router), then the other option is to use a wireless adapter, which can be purchased at most computer electronics stores or online.

Additional Raspberry Pi Features

The Raspberry Pi is a actual computer -- not much different than what's in a laptop computer or a cellphone -- and runs a full-featured Operating System. When you're using the RaspberrySTEM Development Environment (RDE), you're simply running an application on the Raspberry Pi.

But, you can leave the RDE and use other features of the Raspberry Pi as well. In this section, we'll talk about how to leave the RDE (and how to get back to it), as well as some of the things you can do outside the RDE.

How to Get Out of the RDE

When you exit from the RDE, you will be taken to a "desktop" environment that looks similar to what you'd find on a typical desktop or laptop computer. From here, you will see some clickable icons and pull-down menus. This is the Raspian desktop, and it provides an interface to many of the other features of the Raspberry Pi.

How to Get Back to the RDE

Once your on the Raspian desktop, you'll see an icon labeled, "RaspberrySTEM (RDE)". If you double-click on that icon, it will bring you back to the RDE right where you left off.
