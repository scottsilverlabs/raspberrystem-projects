YOUR FIRST CIRCUIT

In this project, you’re going to create your first electric circuit. But, before we do that, some introductory discussion would probably be helpful. Let’s talk about some of the basic of electricity, circuits and how they work.


Your First Circuit

Now that we’ve spent a good bit of time talking about electricity and circuits, let’s jump into a real-life example of building a circuit. We’ve provided all parts you will need to build this circuit as part of the RaspberrySTEM kit, so follow along!

The purpose of this circuit to power and illuminate a “light emitting diode (or “LED” for short). An LED a light source that looks and works a bit like a lightbulb, so this circuit will work nearly identically to the circuit pictured above with the battery and the lightbulb.

To build this circuit, you’ll need the following components (all provided in the bag marked "Your First Circuit RaspberrySTEM Cell"):



  1. 3 Volt Battery

  2. 100 Ohm Resistor

  3. Red LED

By the way, remember how we mentioned earlier that sometimes we want to provide extra resistance to a circuit to get it to work? Notice that we have included a resistor in this circuit – its job is to reduce the amount of electricity (current) flowing through the circuit so that the LED doesn’t burn out.

Okay, let’s give this a try, step by step…

Step #1:  Connect the Resistor and LED to the Battery

Using your fingers, pinch one lead of the resistor (a "lead" is the metal wire on one side) to the positive (+) side of the battery and pinch the shorter lead of the LED (one of the leads is longer than the other) to the negative (-) side of the battery.
