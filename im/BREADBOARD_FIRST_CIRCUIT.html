BREADBOARDING YOUR FIRST CIRCUIT

In our previous project (Your First Circuit), we built the following circuit using a battery, an LED and a resistor:


Lighting an LED Diagram


We’re now going to build that same circuit again, but this time using the breadboard on our RaspberrySTEM. Note that all future projects will use the breadboard as well -- if you haven't read our Using The Breadboard tutorial, we highly recommend you do so now.

Let’s look back at our example schematic from our first circuit:



Lighting an LED Schematic


To implement that design on our breadboard, we would follow the schematic from power (the + terminal of the battery) to ground (the - terminal of the battery), inserting each component along the way. 

Let’s look at this step-by-step:

Step #1:  Provide power and ground to the breadboard.

The first step is to provide power and ground to the breadboard. To do this, you'll need the 3V battery we used in the last project, as well as the two bare wires and the two round adhesive dots included in the "Your First Circuit RaspberrySTEM Cell" bag. 

....

This is done by wiring the (+) terminal of the 3V battery to one of the holes in the red row along the rail. This will provide power to the entire red row, as all the holes in that row are connected.

Next, wire the (-) terminal of the 3V battery to one of the holes in the blue row along the rail. This will provide ground to the entire blue row, as all holes in that row are connected.

Here’s what the breadboard should look like at this point (with the corresponding portion of the schematic):


Lighting an LED Partial Schematic




Lighting an LED Partial Diagram


Step #2:  Bring power to one of the connect strips on the breadboard.

Next, we will connect power from the red rail to one of the connect strips on the breadboard (the connect strips are where we’ll be creating the heart of the circuit). We do this by running a wire from the power row (the red row along the rail) to any connect strip, and firmly planting the ends of the wire into their respective holes. Remember, it doesn’t matter which hole you choose in the power row, as the entire row is connected. And it doesn’t matter which connect strip you choose or which hole of the connect strip you choose.

Here’s what the breadboard should look like at this point (with the corresponding portion of the schematic):

Lighting an LED Partial Diagram


  1. Connect the resistor.

The next step is to connect the resistor to the breadboard. We do this by inserting one end of the resistor into the same connect strip as the wire. We then insert the other end of the resistor into a different connect strip. Because all five holes in the connect strip are connected, there is now continuity all the way from the power source (the battery) to the first connect strip, through the resistor, and to the second connect strip.

Here’s what the breadboard should look like at this point (with the corresponding portion of the schematic):

Lighting an LED Partial Diagram


  1. Provide continuity from the resistor to new connect strip.

We now place a wire from the same connect strip as the resistor to a new connect strip. This new connect strip is where we will connect the first lead of our LED.

Here’s what the breadboard should look like at this point (with the corresponding portion of the schematic):


Lighting an LED Partial Schematic



Lighting an LED Partial Diagram


  1. Connect the LED.

Now that we have continuity from the resistor to a new connect strip, it’s time to insert the next component – the LED. We insert one end of the LED anywhere in the connect strip we just wired. And we insert the other end of the LED into a new connect strip.

Note that LEDs must be oriented the correct way for electricity to flow through them, so ensure that the long side of the LED is the side inserted into second connect strip.

Here’s what the breadboard should look like at this point (with the corresponding portion of the schematic):


Lighting an LED Partial Schematic


Lighting an LED Partial Diagram



  1. Complete the circuit.

We now have continuity all the way from power, through the resistor and to the LED – it’s now time to complete the circuit by attaching the LED to ground. We do this by attaching the second leg of the LED to the blue row along the rail (the ground row). Because the second leg of the LED is embedded in a connect strip, we can accomplish this by connecting any part of that connect strip to any hole in the blue row using a wire.

Now that the circuit is completed, electricity should be flowing, and your LED should be lit!
